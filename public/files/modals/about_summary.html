About Me

I spent years in operations — managing teams, running day-to-day, making calls under pressure with whatever information was available. That was usually not enough. Reports came late, metrics were inconsistent, and the data that existed was rarely in a shape you could actually act on.

I kept hitting the same wall: the answers were somewhere in the data, but getting to them required either waiting on someone else or accepting the guess. I stopped accepting the guess.

I taught myself the tools — SQL, Python, dbt, BigQuery, the works — and started building the systems I wished had existed. What started as a frustration became a practice, and that practice became this portfolio.

How I Work

I start from the business question, not the dataset. Most data problems aren't really data problems — they're clarity problems. Once the question is sharp, the rest is engineering: clean pipelines, validated transforms, and outputs that tell a story a decision-maker can actually use.

I care about the whole system. Not just the query that works today, but the pipeline that holds up at 3am when something upstream changes. Automated tests. Documented transforms. Stakeholder-ready outputs that don't require a translator.

And increasingly, I care about what comes next — AI-powered automation, agentic systems, and the layer of intelligence that turns good data infrastructure into something that actually acts on what it finds.

What I'm Building Toward

I'm building a freelance analytics practice while pursuing full-time data analyst and analytics engineer roles. Whether I'm joining a team or partnering with a business, I bring the same thing: operational judgment, technical rigor, and a genuine interest in making the data actually useful — not just present.

The projects in this portfolio aren't demos for their own sake. They're the systems I built to learn by doing, and they reflect how I'd approach real problems.
